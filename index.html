Why Pediatric Patients Face Higher Risks in Care

Children Are Not Mini Adults

Lack of Pediatric-Specific Devices

Most airway management devices available today are designed for adults and resized for children.

Unique and Complex Anatomy

Children's anatomy differs greatly from adults, requiring specially designed devices for safe treatment.

Addressing the Pediatric Care Gap

Limited Number of Trained Professionals

There is a shortage of healthcare professionals trained in pediatric airway management, increasing the risks.

Lack of Trained Specialists

Many pediatric intubations in Brazil are performed by non-specialists or by professionals not trained in pediatric intubation.

Our Impact

Market Gap

  • The laryngoscope, although widely used and low-cost, has a success rate of less than 50% on the first attempt.
  • The videolaryngoscope is the gold standard but is expensive, making it inaccessible for public healthcare in countries like Brazil.
  • The laryngeal mask (fasttrack) is a cost-effective solution but is not available for pediatric use.

The gap lies in developing a device that enables quick, effective intubation with minimal training and low cost.
